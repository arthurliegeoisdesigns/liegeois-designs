# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Founders, C-suite executives, and brand leaders who need high-stakes presentations — investor decks, keynotes, board meetings, sales narratives. They arrive with something important to say and need a designer who can make it land. They evaluate Arthur's site the way they evaluate a presentation: does it command the room?

## Product Purpose

Portfolio and studio site for Liégeois Designs, a one-person strategic presentation design practice. The site must do three things: demonstrate mastery through the work itself, establish Arthur as a strategist (not just a designer), and convert serious prospects into conversations. Success looks like a visitor who arrives skeptical and leaves convinced this is the person for their next high-stakes moment.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, Strategic, Commanding. The site should feel like watching a well-directed keynote — every element earns its place, nothing is decorative filler. Confident without arrogance; premium without pretension.

## Anti-references

- Generic agency portfolio templates (stock photo grids, "We're a creative agency" hero, 3-column service cards)
- Overly techy/developer aesthetics (terminal UIs, monospace-everything, startup-bro energy)
- Minimalist to a fault (all-white, text-only, no personality or visual ambition — the work is visual, the site must be too)
- Behance/Dribbble gallery layouts (thumbnail grids with no narrative structure)

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell** — The portfolio is the proof. Let the work speak through immersive, full-bleed presentation rather than self-congratulatory copy.
2. **Strategic clarity** — Every section answers a question the prospect has. Structure mirrors a presentation: hook, evidence, credibility, call to action.
3. **Cinematic restraint** — Motion, glow, and depth effects serve atmosphere, not spectacle. The dark theme is a stage, not a costume.
4. **Expert confidence** — Typography and layout choices should feel deliberate and assured, like someone who knows exactly what they're doing.
5. **Practice what you preach** — A presentation designer's site must itself be an exceptional presentation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance minimum
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected throughout (all animations have reduced-motion fallbacks)
- Sufficient contrast ratios on dark backgrounds (body text ≥ 4.5:1)
- Semantic HTML structure, keyboard navigable, screen-reader friendly
